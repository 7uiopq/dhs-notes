What

It's 10pm, a day before your major exam. Stationery, check. Conducive environment, check. Focus, check. Notes, check. But wait, where's that booklet of science notes that you had meticulously prepared about cells? You frantically search your room, leaving no pillow unturned. Before you know it, half an hour has passed, and you still can't find your notes.

Frazzled with exhaustion, you boot up your trusty computer and try to search online for DHS notes. Hm, what's this? You can't find any? Even after 30 minutes of incessant scrolling?

Welp, at this point, you've wasted an hour without any productivity.


Why

Does the above scenario seem familiar to you? Do you possess uncanny amounts of procrastination and untidiness that you're ashamed of because I'm calling you out for it right now? Have no fear, because that's exactly what this website serves to overcome!

Gone are the days of flipping through hundreds of worksheets that your teachers assigned you in order to just grab one page of notes! Gone are the days of wasting hours upon hours organising your notes and developing massive headaches!

With this website and a public Github repository, you can contribute your own notes to this website by creating a pull request! Or even better, you can go ahead and mooch off the hard work of others by using their notes, because that's what we encourage you to do!

By prioritising the "Serve" in DHS's School Mission, this website aims to serve you with notes of the highest quality; all in one place.


How

"Oi, but I never pay attention in computing class. How do leh?"

Don't fret, it's quite simple! You can always read the README file in the repository, but I'll condense it here for you.

For a short tutorial on contributing, check out this page. Inside, you'll find a link to a tutorial page on how to create a pull request. Before creating a pull request, make sure your contribution follows the Code of Conduct. After contributing, add your name and email here.

And...that's it! That's pretty much all you need to know.


Who, Where, When

Right now, the repository and website is solely maintained by me, foop. However, you can change that! Create a pull request and you can become a contributor!

Where? Well, um. Right here! And all of those other links that were mentioned above and in the homepage!

And when? Whenever you want, but I recommend now.
