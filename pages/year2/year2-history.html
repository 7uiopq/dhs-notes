SBQ Skills



PEEL Paragraphs

  • Point - To answer the question of inquiry and briefly state your views
  • Explanation - To explain the point
  • Example - To provide a real-life example that supports the explanation
  • Link - To link back to the point

Basic Inference

  • PEE format
    • Study the source carefully, consider everything, and make a stand. Do not jump to conclusions
    • Don't fixate on a few words/details, comprehend the full meaning

Inferring Purpose (AMAO)

  • Question Types
    • Why did the author say this?
    • What is the purpose of this source?
    • Why was this published?
  • Format
    • AMAO
      • Author - The person who wrote/published/drew the source
      • Message - The subject matter that the author is trying to convey
      • Audience - The target audience of the source
      • Purpose - The desired impact/what the author wants to the audience to do with the message
    • Identify the tone (bias, exaggeratino) and the aims of the source

Comparison

Two types of questions:

  • Question Type #1
    • In what ways are these sources similar/different?
    • How are these sources similar/different?
    • Provide 2 similarities/differences
  • Question Type #2
    • How similar/different are these two sources?
    • Provide 1 similarity, 1 difference, and 1 evaluation/link

Reliability

Question types can include:

  • How reliable/credible is this source?
  • Do you think this source is fair?
  • Do you believe what this source says?
  • Are you surprised by this source?
  • These two sources give two differing accounts. Is one of them lying?
  • How far does this source help you to decide if this other source was telling the truth?

There are 4 steps when testing reliability:

  1. Identify the claim in the source
    • Determine the claim of the source and question of inquiry
    • Study the source carefully and infer on what it tells you about the topic/issue at hand
  2. Content Accuracy Check using Cross-Reference
    • Do a Cross-Reference (CR) to check the reliability of the content
    • Examine the source closely and point out any contradictions within the source
    • Does the source match/agree with other sources or your contextual/background knowledge
    • Identify the source that you CR to, quote evidence from the source to support your point, and link back to your point
    • When choosing a source for cross-referencing, make sure that it is relevant and reliable
  3. Critically Assessing the Provenance (CAP)
    • Study the provenance carefully and note the:
      • type of source
      • origin of source - who produced the source?
      • purpose of source - why was it produced?
    • Determine whether there are biases/one-sided viewpoints/hidden agendas/generalisations/sweeping statements that affect the reliability of the source
  4. Conclusion
    • Evaluate the extent of reliability of the source based on your accuracy check and CAP results


Timeline



Age of Colonialism

1750-1850: Industrial Revolution

Before 1819: Singapore was a busy port for trade, but filtered into obscurity

1819: Sir Stamford Raffles sets up trading port

1826: Singapore, Malacca and Penang become British colony of the Straits Settlements

1867: Straits Settlements become crown colony

1869: Suez Canal opens, trade booms

1914-1918: World War I

1929: Wall Street Crashes, Great Depression


Japanese Occupation and Political Instability

December 1941: World War II, Japan bombs Singapore

1942: Singapore falls to Japan, which renames it Syonan (Light of the South)

1945: Japan defeated, Singapore under British military administration

1946: Singapore becomes separate crown colony

1947: Year of Strikes

1948: Elections

1950: Maria Hertogh Riots

1954: Anti-National Service Riots

1955: Elections (Rendel Constitution), Hock Lee Bus Riots

1956: Students' Riot

1959: Self-government attained with Lee Kuan Yew as Prime Minister

1963: Merger occurs, Singapore joins the Federation of Malaya, Sabah (North Borneo), and Sarawak in the Federation of Malaysia

1964: Racial Riots

1965: Separation occurs, Singapore separated from Malaysia and became independent
