Year 1 History

History


It's 10pm, a day before your major exam. Stationery, check. Conducive and quiet environment, check. Focus, check. Notes, che... wait a minute, where's that set of chemistry notes that you had meticulously prepared about? You frantically search your room, leaving no pillow unturned. Before you know it, half an hour has passed, and you still can't find your notes.

Frazzled with exhausation, you boot up your trusty computer and try to search online for notes. Hm, what's this? You can't find any? Even after 30 minutes fo incessant scrolling?

Welp, at this point, you've wasted an hour without any productivity. It's likely that you'll only get some minimal hours of sleep with no preparation at all.



Does the above scenario seem familiar to you? Or do you stay up till 3am just looking at your phone without even studying? Or perhaps you possess uncanny amounts of procrastination and unpreparedness that you're ashamed of because I'm calling you out for it right now? Have no fear, because that's exactly what this website serves to overcome!

Gone are the days of flipping through hundreds of daunting worksheets that your teachers assigned you in order to just gran one page of notes! Gone are the days of wasting hours upon hours trying to organising your notes and developing massive migraines! With this website and a public Github repository, you can contribute your own notes to this website by creating a pull request! Or even better, you can go ahead and mooch off the hard work of others by using their notes, because that's what we encourage you to do!

By prioritising the 'Serve" in DHS's School Mission, this website aims to serve you with notes of the highest quality: all in one place, hassle-free.



"Oi, but I never pay attention in computing class. How do leh?" - you probably

Don't fret, it's quite simple! You can always read the README file in the repository, but I'll condense it here for you.

For a short tutorial on contributing, check out this page. Inside, you'll find a link to a tutorial page on how to create a pull request. Before creating a pull request, make sure your contribution follows the Code of Conduct. After contributing, add your name and Github URL at either here for note contributions, or here for web contributions.

And...that's it! That's pretty much all you need to know.
